# Workshop 7 · Module 1 — Homework
## Narrative Structure in Data Communication

**Due**: Before Workshop 7, Module 3
**Format**: R script + Python script + PDF report (max 6 pages)
**Weight**: Part of Workshop 7 homework (5% of total grade)

### Part A — Big Idea Statement (15 points)
Choose ONE of: Netflix dataset or e-Car dataset. Write three different Big Idea statements, each targeting a different audience: (a) a content strategist at Netflix / a risk officer at the bank, (b) a data science team, (c) a general public blog post. Each Big Idea must follow the structure: "[Subject] should [action] because [evidence]." In 100 words, explain how the audience changes the framing.

### Part B — Exploratory vs Explanatory (25 points)
Take one finding from your W06 homework (any module). Produce two versions of the same chart: (a) the **exploratory** version (as you originally made it: descriptive title, legend, all data visible), (b) the **explanatory** version (declarative title, grey+accent, direct labels, annotation). Produce in both R and Python. In 150 words, itemise every change you made and explain why each change serves the audience.

### Part C — Three-Slide Story Arc (35 points)
Build a three-chart sequence that follows the story arc: (1) Context (establish the baseline — e.g., cumulative catalog growth), (2) Complication (the problem — e.g., movie decline), (3) Resolution (the opportunity — e.g., genre growth engines). Each chart must have a declarative title that IS the finding. Compose the three charts as a vertical 3×1 panel. Produce in both R (patchwork) and Python (subplots). In 200 words, annotate each slide: what is the narrative function? Why this chart type? What should the audience conclude?

### Part D — Descriptive vs Declarative Titles (25 points)
Take five charts from your W04–W06 homework. For each, write: (a) the original descriptive title, (b) a rewritten declarative title. Present as a two-column table. In 100 words, explain: which title style is appropriate for a dashboard? Which for a board presentation? Why?
