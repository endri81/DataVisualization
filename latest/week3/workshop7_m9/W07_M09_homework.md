# Workshop 7 · Module 9 — Homework
## Audience Adaptation

**Due**: Before Workshop 7, Module 10 (Lab)
**Format**: R script + Python script + PDF report (max 8 pages)
**Weight**: Part of Workshop 7 homework (5% of total grade)

### Part A — Three Audience Variants (40 points)
Choose ONE finding from your W06 or W07 work. Produce THREE chart variants of the same finding for three audiences: (a) executive (grey+accent, declarative title, one callout, no legend), (b) technical (multiple series, CI ribbon or error bars, descriptive title, legend), (c) public (simple bar/area, conversational title, big labels, no jargon). Produce all three in both R and Python (6 charts total). Compose as a 1×3 panel for comparison.

### Part B — Adaptation Table (20 points)
For your three variants from Part A, fill in a 6-row table showing how each of the six dimensions (title style, chart complexity, annotations, legend, jargon, CTA) changes across the three audiences. In 100 words, explain which dimension required the most adaptation effort.

### Part C — Medium Variants (20 points)
Take your EXECUTIVE variant and adapt it for two different media: (a) a slide projected in a meeting room (increase font sizes, simplify), (b) a social media post (vertical aspect ratio, 3-second readability, bold). Produce both in R or Python. In 100 words, compare: what information is lost when adapting from a report to social media?

### Part D — e-Car Audience Exercise (20 points)
Produce a 1×3 audience comparison panel for ONE e-Car finding (executive, technical, public). Each variant must adjust all six dimensions appropriately. In 100 words, explain how the financial domain changes the adaptation (e.g., what jargon is acceptable for executives in finance vs entertainment?).
