# Workshop 7 · Module 7 — Homework
## Case Study: Netflix Data Story

**Due**: Before Workshop 7, Module 9
**Format**: R script + Python script + PDF report (max 10 pages)
**Weight**: Part of Workshop 7 homework (5% of total grade)

### Part A — Big Idea + Storyboard (20 points)
Write ONE Big Idea statement for a Netflix data story targeting a content strategy team. Then create a 10-slide storyboard table: for each slide, specify (a) the assertion headline, (b) the story type (M04), (c) the chart type, (d) the arc position (context/complication/analysis/resolution/CTA). No charts needed yet — just the plan.

### Part B — Build 6 Evidence Slides (40 points)
From your storyboard, produce the 6 most important evidence slides (slides 2–7 or equivalent) as fully annotated charts: assertion-evidence layout, grey+accent, direct labels, callouts. Produce in both R and Python (12 charts). Each chart should use the clean theme from M06. Compose as a 3×2 panel for the report.

### Part C — Title Sequence Test (15 points)
List your 10 slide titles in sequence. In 100 words, evaluate: does the narrative flow logically? Are there gaps (missing transitions)? Revise any titles that fail the "cover the chart" test.

### Part D — Colour + Design Consistency (15 points)
Describe your colour language (which colour = which meaning) and verify it is consistent across all 6 charts. In 100 words, explain: why does consistency across slides matter? What happens if each slide uses a different colour scheme?

### Part E — Reflection (10 points)
In 200 words, reflect: what was the hardest part of converting EDA findings into a narrative? Which W07 technique (story arc, assertion headlines, grey+accent, declutter) had the biggest impact on your deck's clarity?
