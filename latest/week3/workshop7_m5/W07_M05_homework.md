# Workshop 7 · Module 5 — Homework
## Assertion-Evidence Slide Design

**Due**: Before Workshop 7, Module 7
**Format**: R script + Python script + PDF report (max 8 pages)
**Weight**: Part of Workshop 7 homework (5% of total grade)

### Part A — Assertion Headlines (20 points)
Take 5 charts from your W04–W06 work. For each, write: (a) the original topic title (if any), (b) a rewritten assertion headline (complete sentence, <15 words), (c) a one-sentence explanation of what the headline communicates that the topic title did not. Present as a 5-row table.

### Part B — 5-Slide Deck (40 points)
Build a 5-slide assertion-evidence deck on the Netflix OR e-Car dataset:
- Slide 1: Context (assertion + cumulative/overview chart)
- Slide 2: Complication (assertion + change/contrast chart)
- Slide 3: Analysis (assertion + factor/drill-down chart)
- Slide 4: Resolution (assertion + recommendation chart)
- Slide 5: Call to Action (assertion + specific targets)

Each slide = one assertion headline (<15 words) + one full-width annotated chart. No bullet points anywhere. Produce all 5 charts in both R and Python. Compose as a 5×1 vertical panel for the PDF.

### Part C — Progressive Reveal (20 points)
For ONE of your 5 slides, produce the 4-build progressive reveal: (a) frame only (axes), (b) + data, (c) + annotation, (d) + assertion headline. Arrange as a 2×2 panel. In 100 words, explain: how does progressive reveal change the audience's experience compared to showing the complete slide at once?

### Part D — Peer Critique (20 points)
Find a real data presentation online (SlideShare, conference talk, corporate report). In 200 words, evaluate: (a) does it use topic titles or assertion headlines? (b) does it use bullet points or evidence visuals? (c) rewrite 2 of its slide titles as assertion headlines. (d) what one change would most improve its effectiveness?
