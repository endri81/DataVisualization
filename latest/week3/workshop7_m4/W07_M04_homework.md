# Workshop 7 · Module 4 — Homework
## The Seven Basic Data Stories

**Due**: Before Workshop 7, Module 6
**Format**: R script + Python script + PDF report (max 6 pages)
**Weight**: Part of Workshop 7 homework (5% of total grade)

### Part A — Story Classification (20 points)
Take SEVEN findings from your W04–W06 work (across both Netflix and e-Car datasets). For each finding, identify: (a) the exact finding sentence (declarative), (b) the keywords that signal the story type, (c) the story type (1–7), (d) the chart type you would use. Present as a 7-row table.

### Part B — One Story, One Chart (35 points)
Choose THREE of your seven findings from Part A (representing three different story types). For each, produce a single fully-annotated explanatory chart: declarative title, grey+accent or highlight, direct labels, callout if needed. Produce in both R and Python (6 charts total). Each chart should carry ONE message and ONE message only.

### Part C — Story Arc Sequence (25 points)
Arrange your three charts from Part B into a story arc: (1) Context or Zoom Out, (2) Complication (Change or Contrast), (3) Resolution (Factors or Drill Down). Compose as a vertical 3×1 panel. In 150 words, explain: why this order? How does each chart advance the narrative?

### Part D — Outlier Story (20 points)
Produce a chart that tells an Outlier story from either dataset: identify 2–3 extreme observations, highlight them with colour and annotation, and explain in 100 words why they are unusual and what they might indicate.
